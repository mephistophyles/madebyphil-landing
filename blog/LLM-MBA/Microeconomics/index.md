Given the depth and complexity of microeconomic theory and its applications, let's split it into 2 courses: Microeconomics I (Foundations and Market Structures) and Microeconomics II (Strategic Behavior and Advanced Topics).

# Microeconomics I: Foundations and Market Structures

## Module 1: Introduction to Microeconomic Analysis

**Core Concepts:**
- Scarcity, choice, and opportunity cost fundamentals
- Comparative advantage and gains from specialization
- Economic methodology: Positive vs. normative analysis
- Marginalist revolution and its impact on economic thinking
- Mathematical foundations: Optimization, equilibrium, comparative statics
- Microeconomic models: Assumptions, limitations, and applications
- Markets and price system as coordination mechanisms
- Evolution of microeconomic thought: Classical to contemporary approaches

**Case Discussion:** "The Seattle Minimum Wage Study" - Examine how economists applied microeconomic methods to analyze the impacts of Seattle's minimum wage increase, highlighting methodological challenges in empirical microeconomics.

## Module 2: Consumer Theory and Demand Analysis

**Core Concepts:**
- Utility theory: Cardinal and ordinal approaches
- Budget constraints and feasible consumption sets
- Consumer optimization and demand derivation
- Income and substitution effects of price changes
- Slutsky equation decomposition
- Consumer surplus measurement
- Revealed preference theory
- Behavioral economics extensions: Prospect theory, bounded rationality, hyperbolic discounting

**Case Discussion:** "Netflix's Price Discrimination Strategy" - Analyze how Netflix designs its subscription tiers and pricing structure based on consumer preference heterogeneity and willingness to pay.

## Module 3: Theory of the Firm and Production Analysis

**Core Concepts:**
- Production functions: Cobb-Douglas, CES, Leontief forms
- Short-run vs. long-run production decisions
- Returns to scale and economies of scope
- Cost minimization and input demand derivation
- Short-run cost functions: Fixed, variable, average, marginal costs
- Long-run cost curves and planning horizons
- Learning curves and dynamic cost reduction
- Production theory and technological change

**Case Discussion:** "Toyota's Production System" - Examine how Toyota's manufacturing approach embodies theoretical principles of cost minimization, learning effects, and production efficiency.

## Module 4: Perfect Competition and Efficiency

**Core Concepts:**
- Characteristics of perfectly competitive markets
- Short-run firm supply curve derivation
- Long-run equilibrium and zero economic profit condition
- Industry supply curve construction
- Producer surplus measurement
- Pareto efficiency concept
- First welfare theorem and market efficiency
- Deadweight loss and market interventions

**Case Discussion:** "Agricultural Commodity Markets" - Analyze how competitive market theory explains price determination and adjustment in global corn or wheat markets.

## Module 5: Monopoly and Price Discrimination

**Core Concepts:**
- Single-price monopoly profit maximization
- Deadweight loss of monopoly
- Natural monopoly regulation approaches
- First-degree (perfect) price discrimination
- Second-degree price discrimination (nonlinear pricing)
- Third-degree price discrimination (market segmentation)
- Two-part tariffs and other pricing strategies
- Intertemporal price discrimination

**Case Discussion:** "Airline Dynamic Pricing" - Examine how airlines implement sophisticated price discrimination strategies based on customer segments, booking times, and demand patterns.

## Module 6: Monopolistic Competition and Product Differentiation

**Core Concepts:**
- Characteristics of monopolistically competitive markets
- Chamberlin's theory and market equilibrium
- Product differentiation: Horizontal vs. vertical
- Spatial competition models: Hotelling linear city
- Salop's circular city model
- Monopolistic competition with heterogeneous firms
- Advertising in monopolistically competitive markets
- Multi-product firms and brand proliferation

**Case Discussion:** "The Craft Beer Industry" - Analyze how product differentiation and branding strategies drive competitive dynamics in the rapidly evolving craft beer market.

## Module 7: Oligopoly and Strategic Interaction

**Core Concepts:**
- Market concentration measures: HHI, concentration ratios
- Cournot quantity competition model
- Bertrand price competition with homogeneous goods
- Stackelberg leader-follower model
- Capacity constraints and Edgeworth cycles
- Contestable markets theory
- Limit pricing and entry deterrence
- Tacit collusion and facilitating practices

**Case Discussion:** "The OPEC Cartel's Stability Challenges" - Examine the strategic interactions among oil-producing countries and the difficulties in maintaining cartel discipline.

## Module 8: General Equilibrium and Welfare Economics

**Core Concepts:**
- Partial vs. general equilibrium analysis
- Edgeworth box and contract curve
- Walrasian equilibrium in exchange economies
- Production and consumption in general equilibrium
- Second welfare theorem and market intervention
- Social welfare functions and distributive justice
- Arrow's impossibility theorem
- Market failure categorization framework

**Case Discussion:** "Carbon Tax Design and Economy-wide Effects" - Analyze how general equilibrium approaches help evaluate the comprehensive impacts of carbon pricing across different sectors.

## Module 9: Market Failure: Externalities and Public Goods

**Core Concepts:**
- Positive and negative externalities: Classification and examples
- Coase theorem and property rights solutions
- Pigouvian taxes and subsidies
- Cap-and-trade systems for externality management
- Pure public goods: Non-rivalry and non-excludability
- Free-rider problem and voluntary provision
- Public goods provision mechanisms
- Commons problems and common-pool resources

**Case Discussion:** "Urban Congestion Pricing" - Examine how cities like London and Singapore have implemented pricing schemes to address the externalities of urban traffic congestion.

# Microeconomics II: Strategic Behavior and Advanced Topics

## Module 1: Game Theory Foundations

**Core Concepts:**
- Strategic form games and Nash equilibrium
- Dominant and dominated strategies
- Pure vs. mixed strategy equilibria
- Extensive form games and backward induction
- Subgame perfect equilibrium
- Repeated games and folk theorems
- Games with incomplete information
- Evolutionary game theory foundations

**Case Discussion:** "Pricing Strategies in the Smartphone Industry" - Analyze competitive interactions between Apple and Samsung through game-theoretic models of product release timing and pricing.

## Module 2: Information Economics: Uncertainty and Risk

**Core Concepts:**
- Expected utility theory and risk preferences
- Value of information under uncertainty
- State-preference approach to uncertainty
- Insurance markets and risk pooling
- Asset pricing fundamentals
- Risk measures and stochastic dominance
- Prospect theory and framing effects
- Ambiguity aversion and Ellsberg paradox

**Case Discussion:** "Catastrophe Insurance Markets" - Examine how insurance providers price and manage exposure to low-probability, high-impact events like hurricanes or earthquakes.

## Module 3: Information Economics: Asymmetric Information

**Core Concepts:**
- Adverse selection mechanisms
- Signaling models: Spence's education signaling
- Screening approaches to adverse selection
- Market unraveling with adverse selection
- Moral hazard in principal-agent relationships
- Incentive contract design
- Multi-task principal-agent problems
- Team production and free-riding

**Case Discussion:** "Health Insurance Market Design" - Analyze how the Affordable Care Act attempted to address adverse selection problems in health insurance markets.

## Module 4: Auction Theory and Mechanism Design

**Core Concepts:**
- Private value auction formats: English, Dutch, first-price, second-price
- Revenue equivalence theorem
- Common value auctions and winner's curse
- Optimal auction design
- Multi-unit auctions and combinatorial auctions
- Mechanism design principles: Revelation principle
- Strategy-proof mechanisms
- Vickrey-Clarke-Groves mechanisms

**Case Discussion:** "Spectrum Auction Design" - Examine how governments design auctions for allocating wireless spectrum rights to telecommunications companies.

## Module 5: Behavioral Economics

**Core Concepts:**
- Bounded rationality models
- Reference dependence and loss aversion
- Hyperbolic discounting and time inconsistency
- Mental accounting and narrow framing
- Social preferences: Fairness, reciprocity, altruism
- Heuristics and biases in decision-making
- Choice architecture and nudge theory
- Neuroeconomics foundations

**Case Discussion:** "Retirement Savings Plan Design" - Analyze how behavioral insights have transformed retirement plan structures through features like auto-enrollment and default options.

## Module 6: Labor Economics

**Core Concepts:**
- Labor supply decisions: Income-leisure tradeoff
- Human capital investment theory
- Compensating differentials in wage determination
- Efficiency wage models
- Labor market discrimination theories
- Monopsony in labor markets
- Search and matching models
- Minimum wage effects: Theoretical perspectives

**Case Discussion:** "Gig Economy Labor Markets" - Examine how platforms like Uber and Task Rabbit have created new labor market structures with distinctive microeconomic properties.

## Module 7: Industrial Organization and Regulation

**Core Concepts:**
- Market structure-conduct-performance paradigm
- Contestable markets theory
- Network effects and platform competition
- Two-sided markets and multi-sided platforms
- Vertical integration theories
- Bundling and tying strategies
- Predatory pricing analysis
- Regulatory design: Rate-of-return vs. price cap regulation

**Case Discussion:** "Google's Antitrust Challenges" - Analyze how economic theories of platform competition, bundling, and network effects inform antitrust scrutiny of Google.

## Module 8: Economics of Innovation and Intellectual Property

**Core Concepts:**
- Innovation incentives and appropriability mechanisms
- Patent races and R&D competition
- Optimal patent design
- Cumulative innovation and sequential innovation
- Technology adoption and diffusion models
- Open innovation paradigms
- Standard setting and compatibility
- Economics of digital goods and intellectual property

**Case Discussion:** "Pharmaceutical Patent Protection" - Examine the tradeoffs between innovation incentives and access in pharmaceutical patent policy.

## Module 9: Market Design and Matching Markets

**Core Concepts:**
- Two-sided matching markets
- Stability in matching markets
- Gale-Shapley deferred acceptance algorithm
- Strategy-proofness in matching mechanisms
- School choice and student assignment systems
- Kidney exchange and market design without money
- Thick markets and market congestion
- Repugnant markets and ethical constraints

**Case Discussion:** "Medical Residency Match Program" - Analyze how the National Resident Matching Program uses market design principles to match medical students with hospital residency positions.

## Module 10: Development Microeconomics

**Core Concepts:**
- Poverty traps and multiple equilibria
- Microfinance models and credit constraints
- Property rights and investment incentives
- Household models and intrahousehold allocation
- Technology adoption in developing countries
- Field experiments in development economics
- Cash transfers: Conditional vs. unconditional
- Behavioral constraints to development

**Case Discussion:** "Randomized Controlled Trials in Development Policy" - Examine the methodology and findings of experimental interventions like microfinance programs or educational incentives.

## Module 11: Environmental and Resource Economics

**Core Concepts:**
- Environmental valuation methods: Revealed and stated preference
- Renewable and non-renewable resource extraction
- Hotelling's rule for resource depletion
- Externality correction mechanisms: Taxes vs. tradable permits
- International environmental agreements
- Discounting and intergenerational equity
- Economics of biodiversity and ecosystem services
- Option value and irreversibility in environmental decision-making

**Case Discussion:** "European Carbon Trading System" - Analyze the design, implementation challenges, and effectiveness of Europe's cap-and-trade program for carbon emissions.

## Module 12: Experimental and Behavioral Game Theory

**Core Concepts:**
- Ultimatum game experiments and fairness
- Public goods games and cooperation
- Trust games and reciprocity
- Coordination games and focal points
- Level-k reasoning and cognitive hierarchy models
- Quantal response equilibrium
- Learning in games
- Psychological game theory: Beliefs, emotions, and intentions

**Case Discussion:** "Corporate Strategic Interactions Through a Behavioral Lens" - Examine how behavioral game theory explains cooperation and competition patterns in oligopolistic industries.
