Problem Set with Solutions
Problem 1: Financial vs. Managerial Accounting
Scenario: You are the newly appointed CEO of a global consumer products company with operations in 15 countries. Your CFO has presented you with two sets of reports: the quarterly financial statements prepared for investors and a detailed internal performance analysis by product line and region.
Questions:

Identify three key differences between these two types of reports and explain why each difference exists.
As CEO, how would you use each type of report differently in your decision-making process?
What limitations might exist in each type of report that you should be aware of?



Problem 2: GAAP vs. IFRS Application
Scenario: Your company, GlobalTech Inc., is considering acquiring a European technology firm that reports under IFRS. Your team has identified several accounting differences that will require analysis.
Questions:

The European company has capitalized €2.5 million in development costs for a new software platform. How would this be treated under U.S. GAAP, and what adjustments might be needed post-acquisition?
The target company uses the revaluation model for its corporate headquarters building, which was revalued upward by €4 million last year. What challenges does this present under GAAP, and how would you address them?
As the acquiring company's CFO, what process would you implement to manage ongoing differences between GAAP and IFRS after the acquisition?




Problem 3: Regulatory Framework Analysis
Scenario: As the CFO of a U.S.-based multinational corporation planning an IPO in the next 18 months, you need to prepare your accounting department for increased regulatory scrutiny.
Questions:

Identify the key regulatory bodies that will oversee your financial reporting after the IPO, and explain their respective roles.
What specific additional financial reporting requirements will apply to your company post-IPO that don't apply as a private company?
Create a high-level implementation plan with key milestones to prepare your accounting function for public company reporting.




Additional financial reporting requirements post-IPO:

Quarterly reporting (Form 10-Q) in addition to annual reports (Form 10-K)
Accelerated filing deadlines (60-90 days for annual reports depending on size)
Management's Discussion and Analysis (MD&A) of financial condition and results
CEO/CFO certifications under Sarbanes-Oxley Sections 302 and 906
Internal control over financial reporting assessment and auditor attestation (SOX 404)
Enhanced disclosures for executive compensation, related party transactions, and risk factors
8-K filings for material events between periodic reports
More comprehensive segment reporting and financial statement footnotes
Proxy statement disclosures for annual shareholder meetings
Earnings release protocols and non-GAAP reconciliation requirements


Implementation plan with key milestones:

18 Months Before IPO:

Assess current accounting policies against public company requirements
Evaluate accounting system capabilities and plan upgrades
Begin preparing 2-3 years of audited financial statements
Identify key personnel needs and begin recruitment


12 Months Before IPO:

Establish disclosure committee and reporting protocols
Draft accounting policy manual compliant with public company standards
Implement enhanced financial closing process with accelerated timeline
Begin SOX 404 readiness assessment and documentation


9 Months Before IPO:

Conduct technical accounting analysis for complex areas (revenue, leases, etc.)
Implement upgraded accounting/ERP system if needed
Develop financial reporting calendar and templates
Begin preparing pro forma financial statements for registration


6 Months Before IPO:

Conduct dry runs of quarterly close process
Implement remediation of identified internal control weaknesses
Train staff on SEC reporting requirements
Begin drafting registration statement financial sections


3 Months Before IPO:

Finalize audited financial statements for registration statement
Complete internal control documentation and testing
Establish investor relations protocols for financial communications
Conduct comprehensive readiness assessment with external auditors


Post-IPO:

Implement ongoing compliance monitoring system
Establish continuous training program for accounting staff
Develop benchmarking against peer company disclosures
Create process for monitoring new accounting pronouncements
