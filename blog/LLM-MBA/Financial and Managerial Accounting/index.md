# Financial and Managerial Accounting: Executive MBA Preparation Syllabus

## Module 1: Types of Accounting Frameworks

**Core Concepts:**
- Financial vs. Managerial Accounting: Fundamental differences in purpose, audience, and reporting requirements
- Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP): Historical development and conceptual framework
- International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS): Global standardization and key differences from GAAP
- Framework for the Preparation and Presentation of Financial Statements: Underlying assumptions and qualitative characteristics
- Regulatory bodies: SEC, FASB, IASB roles in standard-setting

**Case Discussion:** "Global Accounting Standards Convergence: The Tesla Case" - Examine how Tesla navigates reporting requirements across multiple jurisdictions and markets.

## Module 2: The Balance Sheet and Transaction Recording

**Core Concepts:**
- Accounting equation fundamentals: Assets = Liabilities + Equity
- Double-entry bookkeeping system: Debits and credits framework
- Asset classification: Current vs. non-current, tangible vs. intangible
- Liability classification: Short-term vs. long-term obligations
- Equity components: Contributed capital, retained earnings, treasury stock
- Transaction analysis workflow: From business events to financial statements
- Balance sheet presentation requirements and footnote disclosures

**Case Discussion:** "Apple Inc.'s Balance Sheet Evolution" - Analyze how Apple's balance sheet transformed as it shifted from a computer manufacturer to a diverse technology ecosystem company.

## Module 3: The Income Statement and Accrual Accounting

**Core Concepts:**
- Accrual vs. cash basis accounting: Timing recognition principles
- Revenue and expense recognition: Matching principle applications
- Income statement formats: Single-step vs. multi-step presentations
- Comprehensive income reporting: Operating vs. non-operating items
- Earnings quality assessment: Sustainable vs. transitory components
- Earnings management considerations: Accounting discretion and ethics
- Pro forma earnings and non-GAAP metrics: Benefits and limitations

**Case Discussion:** "Amazon's Profitability Journey" - Examine Amazon's historical income statement evolution, focusing on their strategic prioritization of growth over immediate profits.

## Module 4: The Accounting Process

**Core Concepts:**
- Accounting cycle: From transactions to financial statements
- Journal entries: Recording, posting, and adjustment processes
- Trial balance preparation and error detection techniques
- Worksheet development and analysis
- Adjusting entries: Accruals, deferrals, and estimations
- Closing process and preparation for next period
- Internal controls within the accounting cycle
- Accounting information systems: Design considerations

**Case Discussion:** "Salesforce's Accounting System Transition" - Explore how Salesforce managed its accounting processes while scaling from startup to enterprise software leader.

## Module 5: Revenue Recognition

**Core Concepts:**
- Five-step revenue recognition model under ASC 606/IFRS 15
- Contract identification and performance obligations
- Transaction price determination and allocation methodologies
- Revenue timing: Point-in-time vs. over-time recognition patterns
- Multiple-element arrangements and bundled offerings
- Contract modifications and variable consideration
- Industry-specific revenue recognition challenges
- Principal vs. agent considerations

**Case Discussion:** "Netflix's Subscription Revenue Model" - Analyze Netflix's revenue recognition policies for its subscription-based business model, including international complexities.

## Module 6: Inventory and Cost of Goods Sold

**Core Concepts:**
- Inventory cost flow assumptions: FIFO, LIFO, weighted average methods
- Lower of cost or market/net realizable value assessments
- Inventory accounting systems: Periodic vs. perpetual
- Cost of goods sold determination and gross margin analysis
- Manufacturing inventory components: Raw materials, WIP, finished goods
- Inventory turnover metrics and operational efficiency
- Inventory valuation impacts on financial statements
- LIFO reserve and LIFO liquidation effects

**Case Discussion:** "Toyota's Just-in-Time Inventory Management" - Examine how Toyota's inventory philosophy impacts its financial reporting and performance metrics.

## Module 7: Statement of Cash Flows

**Core Concepts:**
- Cash flow categorization: Operating, investing, and financing activities
- Direct vs. indirect methods for operating cash flow reporting
- Non-cash investing and financing disclosures
- Free cash flow calculation and interpretation
- Cash flow quality assessment techniques
- Cash flow statement preparation from balance sheet and income statement data
- Cash flow forecasting methodologies
- Cash flow statement linkage to other financial statements

**Case Discussion:** "Berkshire Hathaway's Cash Flow Management" - Analyze how Warren Buffett's company generates, allocates, and preserves cash across diverse business units.

## Module 8: Long-term Assets and Depreciation

**Core Concepts:**
- Capitalization criteria for long-term assets
- Historical cost principle and subsequent measurement
- Depreciation methods: Straight-line, accelerated, units-of-production
- Useful life estimation and residual value determination
- Component depreciation for complex assets
- Asset impairment testing and write-downs
- Deferred tax implications of depreciation differences
- Asset retirement obligations

**Case Discussion:** "Boeing's 787 Dreamliner Program" - Explore Boeing's capitalization decisions for its complex aircraft development program and subsequent depreciation policies.

## Module 9: Matching Principle for PP&E

**Core Concepts:**
- Capitalization vs. expense decision framework
- Subsequent expenditures: Repairs, maintenance, betterments, adaptations
- Self-constructed asset cost accumulation
- Interest capitalization during construction periods
- Asset component accounting and replacement cycles
- Disposal and exchange transactions
- Specialized industry applications of PP&E matching
- Financial statement disclosure requirements

**Case Discussion:** "ExxonMobil's Oil Field Development" - Analyze how ExxonMobil applies the matching principle to its massive capital-intensive exploration and production assets.

## Module 10: Marketable Securities and Time Value of Money

**Core Concepts:**
- Debt security classification: Trading, available-for-sale, held-to-maturity
- Equity investment accounting approaches
- Fair value measurement hierarchy
- Unrealized gains/losses treatment by security type
- Other comprehensive income components
- Time value of money applications: Present value and future value
- Impairment considerations for investments
- Deferred tax implications of unrealized gains/losses
- Discounted cash flow modeling for valuation

**Case Discussion:** "Berkshire Hathaway's Investment Portfolio" - Examine how Warren Buffett's company accounts for its massive portfolio of marketable securities and strategic investments.

## Module 11: Long-Term Debt

**Core Concepts:**
- Debt issuance: Premium, discount, and issuance costs
- Effective interest method for amortization
- Debt covenants and compliance reporting
- Convertible debt features and accounting implications
- Early extinguishment of debt: Gains and losses
- Troubled debt restructurings
- Foreign currency-denominated debt
- Debt footnote disclosure requirements
- Debt ratios and leverage analysis

**Case Discussion:** "Tesla's Debt Financing Strategies" - Analyze Tesla's complex debt structure, including convertible bonds, and how it reports these obligations.

## Module 12: Leases and Off-Balance Sheet Financing

**Core Concepts:**
- Lease classification: Finance vs. operating under ASC 842/IFRS 16
- Right-of-use asset and lease liability determination
- Lease term assessment including extension options
- Variable lease payments and residual value guarantees
- Sale-leaseback transaction accounting
- Special purpose entities and consolidation principles
- Financial statement impacts of leasing standards
- Disclosure requirements for lease commitments

**Case Discussion:** "Amazon's Fulfillment Center Strategy" - Examine Amazon's approach to leasing its vast network of fulfillment centers and the financial reporting implications.

## Module 13: Current Liabilities and Contingencies

**Core Concepts:**
- Short-term obligation classification and recognition
- Contingent liability recognition criteria: Probable, reasonably possible, remote
- Asset retirement obligations accounting
- Warranty and product liability provisions
- Litigation reserves and disclosure requirements
- Environmental liabilities and remediation costs
- Restructuring charges and exit costs
- Employee-related liabilities: Compensation, benefits, pensions

**Case Discussion:** "BP's Deepwater Horizon Disaster" - Analyze BP's liability recognition and disclosure evolution following the massive Gulf of Mexico oil spill.

## Module 14: Intangibles and Business Combinations

**Core Concepts:**
- Intangible asset classification and recognition criteria
- Goodwill determination in business acquisitions
- Amortization vs. impairment testing approaches
- Business combination accounting: Acquisition method
- Fair value measurement in purchase price allocation
- Contingent consideration and earnout arrangements
- In-process research and development treatment
- Push-down accounting considerations
- Disclosure requirements for business combinations

**Case Discussion:** "Microsoft's Acquisition of LinkedIn" - Examine Microsoft's accounting for this major acquisition, focusing on intangible asset identification and valuation.

## Module 15: Introduction to Cost Concepts

**Core Concepts:**
- Cost behavior patterns: Fixed, variable, mixed, step
- Direct vs. indirect cost classification
- Product vs. period cost distinctions
- Manufacturing cost components: Materials, labor, overhead
- Contribution margin analysis and break-even modeling
- Relevant costs for decision-making
- Sunk and opportunity costs in management decisions
- Activity-based costing implementation
- Performance measurement alignment with strategic objectives

**Case Discussion:** "Southwest Airlines' Cost Structure" - Analyze Southwest's approach to cost management that enables its low-cost carrier positioning in the airline industry.

## Module 16: Indirect Allocation of Costs

**Core Concepts:**
- Cost pool establishment and homogeneity
- Cost driver identification and selection criteria
- Allocation base determination: Volume-based vs. activity-based
- Departmental vs. process-based allocation schemes
- Service department cost allocation: Direct, step-down, reciprocal methods
- Joint cost allocation for multiple products
- Transfer pricing methodologies for internal transactions
- Cost allocation policy development and implementation

**Case Discussion:** "Johnson & Johnson's Shared Services Model" - Examine how J&J allocates costs across its diverse pharmaceutical, consumer, and medical device divisions.

## Module 17: Managerial Accounting Integration

**Core Concepts:**
- Budget development: Operating and capital expenditure planning
- Variance analysis: Price, quantity, efficiency, volume
- Responsibility accounting and performance evaluation
- Balanced scorecard implementation
- Standard costing systems design
- Operational KPI development and monitoring
- Capital budgeting techniques: NPV, IRR, payback period
- Make-or-buy decision frameworks
- Capacity utilization analysis

**Case Discussion:** "Starbucks' Store-Level Profitability Analysis" - Explore how Starbucks uses managerial accounting to evaluate performance across thousands of locations with varying characteristics.
